We have had quite a year! From climbing mountains to dance lessons; it has been a fantastic year. We would love to take you through some of the highlights!

We got married!

We kicked off our bride/groom eras this summer with bachelor/bachelorette parties.
Nicole's Bachelorette was a weekend of wine, crafts, and getting sunburnt on a beach.
Alec's Bachelor party...was exactly the type of mayhem and chaos you might expect from him. A 6 player magic game, Mistborn style dnd, board games and laser tag.

On October 4th, we were able to celebrate with you all and tie the knot! We are so grateful to each and every one of you for being there to support us. We had such an amazing day and are so excited to officially start our lives together.

We honeymooned in Thailand! We visited 3 cities in 10 days - Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and Krabi. In Bangkok, we explored the city and Thai culture, visiting temples and eating as much food as we could (including crickets!). In Chiang Mai, we went on outdoor adventures in the mountains and participated in the traditions of two festivals - Loy Krathong and Yi Peng. In Krabi, we escaped the city and relaxed by the ocean for our final few days, before returning home. We even managed to squeeze in a super quick tour of Istanbul on the way home!

Races & Training

Nicole started getting back in the pool and training with the Worcester Area Master's team this year. She entered a race, swimming a mile across Buzzards Bay and raised money to support clean water in the Bay. She endured the cold water that Alec went in at Maritime. We even put a paddleboard on our wedding registry so Alec can keep up with her while she trains next year! S.o to seamus for buying it for us

With the fall came our Spartan Races. Nicole and Alec both completed trifectas (sprint 5k, super 10k, and beast 21k in a calendar year), Nicole for the first time! We completed the super and sprint on back to back days in Barre, MA in August. In September, we did Spartan's hardest beast - up Killington mountain in Vermont. Alec had an undefeated age group season and was top 10 overall in his three races this year. Nicole placed 3rd in age group at the beast. She got a well deserved cheeseburger after that one.

Community Service Events

We opened the year with our annual Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Day of service through our continued volunteering with the Travis Manion Foundation. For this year, we donated and rucked food for 4 miles to Veterans Inc in Worcester, a non profit that assists Veterans in the area in numerous ways including housing, jobs, and food.

Admittedly, our winter months are less of an adventure type and our next notable excursion wasn't until our annual Earth Day Cleanup in April (also with the Travis Manion Foundation). We cleaned the Middle River by Holy Cross. To say it was appalling how many tires we pulled out on just a small stretch of the river is an understatement. This cleanup was in honor of Alec's late Great Uncle Peanut who passed earlier in the year.
